1. About SolidarityPool

SolidarityPool ("we", "us", "the Platform") is a community mutual-aid service that allows members to build a shared health fund through micro round-up savings and access care funds during medical emergencies. The Platform operates via a web application and a USSD shortcode designed for mobile-money regions in East and Central Africa.

SolidarityPool is not a licensed bank, insurer, or financial institution. It is a technology-facilitated peer-to-peer solidarity mechanism. All funds held are community funds and are not deposits insured by any government body.

2. Eligibility

  • You must be at least 18 years old to register as a member.
  • You must provide a valid mobile number capable of receiving SMS.
  • You must be a human individual — corporate or institutional registration is not permitted for member accounts (healthcare providers may register separately via the Provider Programme).
  • You may only hold one member account. Duplicate accounts may be merged or closed.

3. Member Accounts and PIN Security

On registration you choose a 4-digit PIN that authenticates sensitive actions (care fund requests, repayments, balance checks). You are solely responsible for keeping your PIN confidential. SolidarityPool staff will never ask for your PIN.

If you suspect your account has been accessed without your consent, dial our USSD shortcode or contact us immediately so we can lock your account.

4. Round-Up Savings

When you record a purchase via the USSD round-up feature, the difference between your purchase amount and the next whole unit is split as follows:

  • 70 % — credited to your personal sub-wallet (yours to keep or use for care fund requests)
  • 20 % — credited to your community pool (collective fund)
  • 10 % — platform fee retained by SolidarityPool to cover operations, SMS costs, and infrastructure

Round-up credits are applied instantly. The platform fee portion is non-refundable once a transaction is committed.

5. Care Fund Requests

Members may request care funds for genuine medical expenses. By submitting a request you confirm that:

  • The stated medical need is real and the amount is a reasonable estimate of costs.
  • You will use the funds solely for the medical purpose stated.
  • You accept that three community members ("witnesses") will be asked to verify your request and that their decision is final at the community level (subject to admin review for amounts over UGX 180,000 or emergency requests).
  • Any portion of funds drawn from the community pool that cannot be covered by your sub-wallet balance constitutes "social credit" — a debt owed back to the community. Repaying social credit promptly improves your trust score and future draw ceiling.

6. Trust Score and Draw Ceiling

Your draw ceiling — the maximum you can request from the pool at any one time — is calculated from your trust score (0–1). The trust score reflects repayment history, witness accuracy, community network connections, and activity. SolidarityPool reserves the right to adjust the scoring algorithm to maintain pool sustainability.

7. Social Credit and Repayment

Social credit accrues when you receive pool funds you have not yet repaid. You may repay at any time via the web dashboard or USSD option 8. Failure to repay over an extended period will reduce your trust score and draw ceiling. Persistent default may result in account suspension from care fund requests.

8. Healthcare Providers

Clinics, hospitals, and healthcare practitioners may apply to join the Provider Programme. Approved providers receive a unique provider code and can receive care fund disbursements directly. Providers agree to:

  • Only invoice SolidarityPool for services actually rendered to the identified member.
  • Confirm receipt of payments and mark treatment as started within the provider dashboard.
  • Maintain accurate contact information and notify us of any changes to operating status.
  • Not charge members additional fees beyond the invoiced amount without prior disclosure.

Provider status may be revoked at any time for misconduct, fraudulent invoicing, or failure to meet service standards.
