Privacy Policy

SolidarityPool · Effective date: 1 January 2025 · Last updated: May 2026

Your privacy matters to us. This Policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, how it is used, and your rights over it. SolidarityPool operates primarily in Uganda and complies with the Uganda Data Protection and Privacy Act 2019 (DPPA) and, where applicable, the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

1. Who We Are

SolidarityPool is a community mutual-aid platform operated as a technology service. We act as the data controller for all personal data processed through our web application, USSD shortcode, and related services.

We do not collect biometric data, government ID numbers, or sensitive health diagnosis information. Care fund requests describe a financial need, not a medical record.

3. Legal Basis for Processing

  • Contract performance — processing necessary to operate your account, manage round-ups, and disburse care funds.
  • Legitimate interest — fraud detection, trust scoring, platform security, and pool sustainability.
  • Consent — sending SMS notifications (you may opt out at any time by contacting us or dialling USSD and updating your preferences).
  • Legal obligation — retaining transaction records as required by applicable financial and tax laws.

4. How We Use Your Data

  • Creating and managing your member account.
  • Processing round-up credits and pool contributions.
  • Evaluating care fund requests and selecting community witnesses.
  • Calculating and updating your trust score and draw ceiling.
  • Sending SMS alerts (balance updates, witness assignments, payment confirmations) via Africa's Talking.
  • Facilitating M-Pesa / MTN / Airtel Money top-ups and disbursements.
  • Detecting and preventing fraud, account abuse, and brute-force login attempts.
  • Generating anonymised analytics to improve platform sustainability.

6. Data Retention

  • Active accounts: data is retained for the lifetime of the account.
  • Transaction records: retained for 7 years to comply with financial record-keeping requirements.
  • Login history & audit logs: retained for 2 years for security and dispute resolution purposes.
  • Deactivated / anonymised accounts: after you exercise your right to erasure, personal identifiers are replaced with anonymised tokens; transaction totals are retained in aggregate for financial reporting only.

7. Your Rights

Under the Uganda DPPA 2019 and (where applicable) GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate data.
  • Erasure ("right to be forgotten") — request deletion of your account and personal data. This is processed via the admin panel within 30 days. Note that anonymised transaction aggregates are retained for financial compliance.
  • Restriction — ask us to pause processing while a dispute is resolved.
  • Data portability — request your transaction history in CSV format.
  • Objection — object to processing based on legitimate interest.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us through the member dashboard or via USSD. We will respond within 30 days.

8. Security

We use TLS encryption for all web connections. Passwords/PINs are stored in our database. We recommend not reusing your SolidarityPool PIN elsewhere. Failed login attempts are rate-limited and accounts are locked after repeated failures. Administrative actions are logged in an immutable audit trail.

9. Cookies

The web application uses a single session cookie to maintain your login state. This cookie is essential for the service to function and does not track you across other websites. We do not use advertising cookies or third-party analytics trackers.

10. Children's Privacy

SolidarityPool is not intended for persons under 18. We do not knowingly collect data from minors. If you believe a minor has registered, please contact us immediately.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated by SMS at least 14 days before they take effect. The "last updated" date at the top of this page always reflects the current version.

12. Contact

For privacy enquiries, data access requests, or complaints, contact us through the member dashboard or via our USSD shortcode. If you are unsatisfied with our response, you may lodge a complaint with the Uganda Personal Data Protection Office.
